Junior High School Strands

Science, Technology, and Engineering(STE) Program

The Science, Technology and Engineering (STE) Program is the flagship program for the development of Science, Mathematics, Engineering and Research in Special Science Classes (SSC). The Science Curriculum Framework articulates standards, core competencies, approaches and underlying principles in Science for secondary schools in the country.

Technical-Vocational-Livelihood - Cookery>

TVL-Cookery is a Technical-Vocational-Livelihood (TVL) strand that teaches students the skills needed to work in the culinary arts. The program combines theoretical knowledge with hands-on training in a variety of cooking techniques, food safety, and kitchen management.

Technical-Vocational-Livelihood - ICT

TVL - ICT is a strand in the Technical-Vocational-Livelihood (TVL) track of the K-12 curriculum that focuses on information and communication technology (ICT). The strand teaches students how to use digital technologies to process, store, transmit, and manage information.

Indigenous People Education(IPED)

The Indigenous People's Education (IPEd) Program is DepEd's response to the right of Indigenous Peoples (IP) to basic education that is responsive to their context, respects their identities, and promotes their indigenous knowledge, skills, and other aspects of their cultural heritage.

Special Program in Arts(SPA)

The Special Program in the Arts (SPA) is a program by the Department of Education (DepEd) for high school students with potential or talents in the arts, namely, vocals, instruments, visual arts, theater arts, media arts, and dance.

Special Program in Journalism(SPJ)

The Special program in Journalism (SPJ) is established to deepen the experiences, hone the journalistic skills and competencies of student-writers and to reinforce free and responsible journalism.
This program supports R.A. 7079 (Campus Journalism Act) which mandates the Department of Education “to conduct and implement programs in various aspects of journalism.”
The program is a four-year course in journalism at the junior high school level. It is intended to cultivate the learners’ skills in mass communication, print, online and broadcast media.

Special Program in Sports(SPS)

The Special Program in Sports (SPS) was originally conceived to address the needs of talented students in different sports disciplines and to identify and create a pool of athletes who will be trained to compete in national and international sports competitions.

Basic Education Curriculum

Basic education in the Philippines primarily refers to the compulsory K to 12 Program, wherein every child is required to attend school from kindergarten level at around age 5 years to grade 12 or senior high school at around age 16 -17 years. K-12 Program.

Special Education(SPED)

Special education is a type of education that's designed to meet the needs of students with disabilities, special needs, or individual differences.

Open High School Program(OHSP)

The Open High School Program (OHSP) is a distance learning program in the Philippines that allows students to complete high school outside of a traditional school setting.

Senior High School Strands

Science, Technology, Engineering, and Mathematics (STEM)

STEM is an acronym for science, technology, engineering, and math. These four fields share an emphasis on innovation, problem-solving, and critical thinking. And together they make up a popular and fast-growing industry.

Accountancy, Business, and Management (ABM)

The ABM (Accountancy, Business, and Management) Strand is designed for students who are interested in business, finance, entrepreneurship, and accounting. The curriculum of the ABM strand provides a strong foundation in the principles of business and management, as well as financial and accounting concepts.

Humanities and Social Sciences (HUMSS)

The Humanities and Social Sciences (HUMSS) strand is a Senior High School (SHS) academic track that covers social sciences, languages, and the liberal arts. It's designed for students who are interested in the arts, social sciences, and humanities.

TECHNICAL DRAFTING AND PLUMBING

A "technical drafting and plumbing strand" in SHS refers to a specialization within the Technical Vocational Livelihood (TVL) track where students focus on learning the skills of technical drafting, which involves creating detailed drawings for construction projects, with a specific emphasis on plumbing systems, allowing them to design and plan plumbing layouts on blueprints.

AGRICULTURAL CROPS PRODUCTION

The Agricultural Crops Production strand in Senior High School (SHS) is a Technical Vocational Livelihood (TVL) program that teaches students the skills and knowledge needed to work in the agricultural industry. Students learn how to grow crops, maintain them, and harvest them.

ELECTRICAL INSTALLATION AND MAINTENANCE(EIM)

ELECTRICAL INSTALLATION AND MAINTENANCE (EIM) – the process of making and upkeep electrical property or equipment with a curriculum designed to prepare students with entry level of knowledge and manipulative skills for employment in the electrical industry.

INFORMATION COMMUNICATION TECHNOLOGY (ICT)

ICT is a Senior High School strand that prepares you for a career in Information and Communication Technology. It equips you with advanced skills in computer systems, programming, creating web pages, and basic animation. What you will gain from the ICT Strand: Learn about programming, animation or illustration.

HOME ECONOMICS

The Home Economics (HE) strand in Senior High School (SHS) teaches students job-ready skills for personal and household management. It also prepares students for careers in food and nutrition, hospitality, and more.

SHIELDED METAL ARC WELDING (SMAW)

SHIELDED METAL ARC WELDING (SMAW) – the study of welding metals by learning its essentials, techniques, procedures and safe welding practices.
